About Me

Hi, I am Saikat Sengupta. I am an incoming PhD student in Statistical Science at Duke University, starting in Fall 2026. Currently, I am completing my Master of Statistics at the Indian Statistical Institute (ISI), Kolkata, where I also earned my Bachelor of Statistics.

My research spans the theoretical, methodological, and applied aspects of statistics, machine learning, and optimization. While I have background in various frameworks, my doctoral focus is centered on frequentist and distribution-free approaches. Broadly, my active areas of interest include causal inference, conformal prediction, reinforcement learning, high-dimensional statistics, and change-point detection.

Selected Projects

  • Within-Game Forecasting in T20 Cricket: Developed a time series classification framework based on trend parallelism, representing matches as smooth trend functions estimated via local linear regression.
  • WHO Life Expectancy Data: Built linear and regularized regression models using ANCOVA, LASSO, and Knockoff filters to control false discoveries and identify key socioeconomic health determinants.
  • Fieller-Creasy Problem: Investigated the theoretical and numerical performance of the horseshoe prior for nonlinear, many-to-one functionals of model parameters.
